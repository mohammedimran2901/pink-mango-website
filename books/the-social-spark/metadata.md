## Book Description (Blurb)

**Short blurb (for Amazon, retailers):**

> You want deep friendships. You crave connection. But between the missed texts, the social burnout, the RSD spirals, and the fear that you've accidentally disappeared on everyone again — maintaining a social life with ADHD can feel impossible.
>
> *The Social Spark* is not about becoming more social. It's about understanding how your ADHD brain navigates relationships — and building a social life that works *for* you, not one that exhausts you.
>
> From friendship object permanence and social energy management to boundaries without guilt and recovering from burnout, this book gives you real strategies for every stage of the social arc. Written by Elena Voss, MSc, for women who are tired of performing "normal" long enough to make a friend.

**Long blurb (for website, promotional use):**

> Every friendship starts with a spark. Your ADHD brain is exceptional at that initial connection — you're intuitive, curious, emotionally intense, and capable of a depth of connection that neurotypical social scripts rarely reach. The challenge isn't the spark. It's what comes after.
>
> *The Social Spark* is the second book in the Spark series, following *The Spark Within*, and focuses specifically on the social lives of women with ADHD. Drawing on the latest research and real-world strategies tested by neurodivergent women, this book covers the complete arc of social connection:
>
> - The ADHD Social Brain — why your brain processes friendship differently
> - Making and keeping friends — practical systems for adult friendship
> - Social energy management — pacing, recovery, and saying no without guilt
> - RSD and rejection — understanding and managing rejection sensitive dysphoria
> - Boundaries — three types and how to communicate them
> - Conversations — steering toward depth, managing interruption, graceful exits
> - Social media — using it intentionally with an ADHD brain
> - Burnout and recovery — what to do when you've ghosted everyone again
> - Building community — finding your people and belonging on your terms
>
> Each chapter ends with a Toolkit Summary — actionable strategies you can implement immediately. This isn't theory. It's a practical guide to a richer, more connected social life — on your terms.
