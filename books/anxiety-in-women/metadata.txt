TITLE: The Anxious Woman's Compass: Reclaiming Calm, Confidence, and Control
       in a World That Keeps You on Edge

1. BOOK DESCRIPTION
-------------------
Anxiety is not a personal failure. It is a compass that has been calibrated by a
world that overstimulates, overwhelms, and overburdens women. In The Anxious
Woman's Compass, Elena Voss, MSc, offers a warm, science-informed guide to
understanding why your nervous system is on high alert and how to find your way
back to calm.

This book is for the woman who cancels plans because the thought of socialising
feels unbearable. The woman who rehearses conversations in the shower, who lies
awake at 3am replaying every mistake, who carries tension in her shoulders as if
the weight of the world lives there. It is for the perfectionist, the
people-pleaser, the high-achiever who has never felt good enough.

With 13 chapters covering the nervous system, perfectionism, people-pleasing,
sleep, hormones, boundaries, and more, this book provides practical strategies
grounded in neuroscience and psychology. You will learn to read your nervous
system's signals, break the cycle of anxious overwork, set boundaries without
guilt, and recalibrate your compass toward a life oriented by purpose rather
than fear.

Anxiety may have shaped your story, but it does not have to write the ending.
